The History

From 1956 - To 2024

The word 'Prabha' refferes to Lamp and symbol to lord shiv, illuminated by thousands of electrical bulbs, with an height of 91 feets. Initially it was first constructed by the villagers of kavuru in 1946. Later this culter picked up by some more villages. Key villages are Ameensaheb Palem, Kavuru, Appapuram, Govindapuram, Kammavaripalem, Yedavalli, Maddiralla.., . Ameensaheb Palem Prabha was first constructed in 1956. This year marks its 68th anniversary (2024). Throughout these years, its success has been attributed to the unity among the people. In the early days, there was no electrical illumination for the Prabha. Instead, people adorned it with plastic flowers and colorful cloths. During the vintage years, bulls were used to lift the Prabha. Starting from 2000, tractors replaced the bulls, and now, the current generation employs cranes. Most of the ancient equipment has been replaced by modern machinery, although the cost has gradually increased over time—currently estimated at around 30-35 lakhs. Every year, each farmer contributes a portion of their annual income towards the festival, firmly believing that this act will result in bountiful yields for their crops.The highlight of the festive days is when people enter a trance-like state, fervently chanting the timeless phrase 'Hara Haroo' invoking Lord Shiva. “Om trayambakam yajamahey sugandhim pustim vardhanam. Urvarukmiv bandhnan, mrityohmukshiya mamritat 👁”

How It Started ?

Sundudu, a cattleman, with his wife Kundiri used to live towards the south of the Trikuta hills. They became rich soon after the birth of their first child, a beautiful daughter, Anandavalli(Gollabhama). Slowly she became a great devotee of Lord Shiva and started spending most of her time offering prayers to Lord Shiva at the Old Koteswara Temple located on Rudra hill. Eventually, she lost all interest in her materialistic life and started penance for Sri Koteswara Swamy.She used to visit Rudra hill every day, even during the scorching summer, to offer prayers to the Lord. Pleased with her penance, Jangama Devara appeared before her. Sympathized by her penance, Jangama Devara blessed her to be a pregnant, though she was a spinster. Unmindful of her pregnancy she carried her daily prayers as usual. Stupefied at her deep devotion, he again appeared and told her that she need not take so many troubles to perform pujas and offer prayers, and promised her that he himself would come to her house where she could do her penance and commanded her to proceed towards her home, and that he would follow her, but advised her not to look back even once on her way to home, in spite of whatever happens... About Our Village ?

Ameensaheb Palem popularly known as avisaipalem, famous for Ayurvedic Bone-fracture setting center(PVC Hospital). It is located in palnadu(Dist),AndhraPradesh-522611. Apart from this AmeenSaheb palem prabha is one of the tallest prabha in kotappakona Tirunalla. Here Shivratri is the most biggest festival. The villagers spent almost 50 days for the construction of Prabha. the Prabha is lifted before 2 days of shivaratri with the help of villagers and a Crane. it is plced on a four wheeler chariot, for carrying it to the Destination. It travles about 25km and it takes one day of time to reach Nidhi (kotappakonda).This festival play an important role in uniting people.

Trikutadri ?

Kotappakonda Sri Trikoteswara Swamy Temple, nestled in the scenic hills of Palnadu district, Andhra Pradesh, holds deep spiritual significance. According to the inscriptions, the temple was in existence even before 1172 A.D. Among several kings who ruled the place Sri Krishna Deva Raya gifted large chunks of lands for the maintenance of the temple. Devotees flock here seeking blessings and solace. Legend has it that a devoted lady cursed a crow for disturbing her fruit offerings, resulting in the absence of crows around the temple. The temple, with its three peaks symbolizing Lord Brahma, Lord Vishnu, and Lord Shiva, is also known as Trikutadri. Mahasivarathri is the main festival, attracting lakhs of devotees who carry a towering Prabha (lamp) on bullock carts (In the past). The ancient Kotayya Temple on Rudra Shikharam is revered as the initial abode of Trikoteswara Swamy 🕉️.

The First Day

Two days prior to Shivaratri, the Prabha is ceremoniously lifted by the villagers and carefully placed onto a chariot with the assistance of a crane. As night falls, vibrant cultural programs unfold, drawing people from neighboring villages to partake in this joyous event.

The Next Day

It is the holy night that millions of people eagerly await at Kotappakonda. During this time, the entire place glows with the radiance of countless lights .
"Sarvam sivamayam Jagath👁"

The Final Day

After the Prabha reaches its destination, the following day, villagers gather at night to visit the Prabha, bringing along their newly acquired vehicles and bulls. The festivities also include cultural programs, adding to the joyous atmosphere.
